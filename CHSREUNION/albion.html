The Class of '58!

Fifty years had passed since graduation and it was great fun to reconnect! The Albion High Class of '58 Fiftieth Reunion took place September 19, 20 & 21, 2008. It was also Albion's Festival of the Forks weekend - a celebration of community and Albion history complete with parades, live music from the '50s and '60s and all sorts of fun activities.

This website will continue to be our connection to each other; where we can reconnect with our high school classmates and reminisce about our youth in the Fifties. Everyone has a Personal page, and we hope you'll update your page and send us more pictures.

50th Reunion
We came armed with digital cameras to record the 50th Reunion Party on Saturday night, the Friday Night gathering at Cascarelli's, our 15 minutes of fame in the Festival of the Forks Parade. Check out the photos!

Remember When?
Reminisce about the '50s. How many friends did you sneak into the drive-in in your trunk? Who did you cruise Superior Street with on Saturday night? Read classmates' memories of growing up in Albion. Look at some pictures of us! Oh, the trouble we got into - we can laugh at ourselves now that the statute of limitations has run its course. Share your escapades in the pumpkin patch and more…

To fellow classmates,
Beloved Coach Jerry Sacharski died February 27, 2009 at his home. He was present at the Festival of the Forks parade and many had the privilege of talking and reminiscing with him. For those of you who would like to express your appreciation for his impact on your life, please drop his wife Etola a note at:
1017 Fitch Street,
Albion, Michigan 49224;
phone 517 629 4576.

   

In 2003 Earl Nystrom created the original AHS Class of '58 website as a way of re-establishing contact with old friends and classmates. Thanks to Earl, many of us are back in touch half a century after we graduated and went our separate ways. As many of you know, Earl courageously battled cancer. Sadly, he passed away February 19, 2006. We're all grateful to him for his selfless efforts to bring us together again.

To celebrate our class’ 50 years since graduation, I designed and built this website dedicated to Earl’s memory.
Julie Strickler Smith
